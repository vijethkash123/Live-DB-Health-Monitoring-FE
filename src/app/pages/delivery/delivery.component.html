Welcome to the Transition section, below are the list of activities to be followed in a managed services transition.
Phase: Activity:
Delivery Start-up Project

Objective

To establish the management environment in which the project will be executed.

Description

Performing the Start-up-activity is essential to clearly establishing how the project will be set-up and therefore facilitates the project's execution and control activities. It also facilitates communication and relationships that must be established with the project stakeholders throughout the project's execution.

In all projects where BRILLIO is responsible in whole or in part for the outcome, the project Start-up activity and particularly the preparation of the Project Plan are highly recommended. In working environments where business and project management processes are already documented (e.g. BRILLIO business units and outsourcing services engagements including project management processes), only the key and specific elements of the Project Plan (e.g. project definition, scope, assumptions, organizational structure and work plan) need to be documented. Project scope, size, complexity, risk, strategic value and BRILLIO management requirements should guide the decision to prepare a Project Plan.

If the project is using open source software, review the Open Source Checklist as a reminder of management activities related to open source software usage during the Delivery phase.

The Start-up activity can be performed in the following situations:

  • For project delivery to a client;
  • For BRILLIO's internal projects;
  • For the execution of some phases of the Client Partnership Management Framework, such as the preparation of a proposal, due diligence and transition. These phases are considered and conducted as projects;
  • For a project that is derived from an Outsourcing Services engagement, the project can be part of the contract (e.g. part of the contract schedules or part of an annual work program, open delivery budget), or can arise in the course of the engagement evolution.

The Start-up activity consists of the following sub-activities:

  • Confirm the project work environnement within which will work the project team and will have an implact on the work methods;
  • Preparing the Project Plan, describing the scope, assumptions, management organizational structure, management processes and work plan;
  • Reviewing the document to verify its compliance with previous phases requirements and project management guidelines and obtaining approvals;
  • Confirming the assignment of project team members.

The "Legal Framework for Proposals and Contracts" mentioned in the "References" section will assist you in reviewing the essential clauses of the agreements that are usually signed by BRILLIO. If you need clarification or a better understanding of any particular clause, do not hesitate to contact our in-house legal counsel.

Sub-Activities

Confirm Project Work Environment To confirm that the office space, hardware, software, hard copy files, software directories, and communication tools are established.
Prepare Project Plan To prepare the Project Plan and its related documents.
Review and Approve Project Plan To review the Project Plan in order to verify its compliance with the previous phases' requirements and project management guidelines. To obtain the appropriate approvals for the Project Plan from the client and BRILLIO Management.
Confirm Project Team Assignment To confirm the assignment of the individuals who will execute the project.


Phase: Activity:
Delivery Execute and Control Project

Objective

To conduct the project and control its execution by applying the project management processes defined in the Project Plan.

Description

Following the project planning processes of the Start-up Project activity, this activity covers the management of the actual delivery of the project's outcome: its deliverables. To effectively manage a project, the following steps should be applied according to the defined processes of the project plan:

  • Track deliverables and manage their procurement, team efforts, costs and expenses: track the execution of the project's activities, subcontracted deliverables and their impact on the Project Work Plan and the project budget;
  • Manage risks: track and mitigate the project's risks;
  • Control assignments: control the assignment and de-assignment of project team members;
  • Process project change requests: evaluate the impacts of project change requests, process their approvals and integrate them into the overall project plan;
  • Resolve outstanding issues: identify, track and resolve outstanding issues;
  • Verify deliverable compliance: validate and verify project deliverables against established criteria and requirements and process their approvals;
  • intain project configuration item list: maintain the project list of deliverables and their status;
  • Report to client authority and BRILLIO management: report information on the project's status, as part of communication management processes;
  • Perform milestone reviews and focus synergy sessions: conduct the reviews and synergy sessions according to Project Plan or as needed;
  • Manage human resources: integrate and support project team members;
  • Perform team members' performance appraisals: perform individual performance appraisals as required.

Although the extent of the application of each of these management activities should be guided by the project scope, size, complexity, risk, strategic value and BRILLIO management requirements, applying these processes will ensure proper control over the project's execution, particularly in the area of scope management. Ineffective scope management is recognized as one of the major causes for project failure.

These processes are iterative and will be repeated several times throughout the project (e.g. tracking deliverables, milestone reviews, maintaining the configuration item list). They should also be recognized as an integrated group of processes. For example, an outstanding issue can be resolved by a project change request. This request will be recorded in the Change Request List and the associated deliverables version changes in the Configuration Item List. It should be noted that the order in which they are described is not an indication of the order in which they are actually performed.

These processes support the monitoring of the project's performance, which should be reported regularly to the project management authorities to identify variance from the initial plan. This can help management in taking preventive actions in anticipation of possible problems.

In order to ensure proper understanding of the project's management environment and outcome by all project stakeholders, the Execute and Control Project activity should not start before the Project Plan has been approved (see Start-up Project, Review and Approve Project Plan).

All documents produced (including notes, meeting minutes) and forms completed and signed during the application of the project delivery management processes should be retained in a Project File. This project file represents the repository of project management information that will, at the end of the project, be archived for future reference.

Sub-Activities

Track Deliverables, Efforts and Cost To maintain the project's budget and work plan.
Manage Risks To track and mitigate project risks..
Control Assignments To control the assignment and de-assignment of team members.
Process Project Change Requests To evaluate and process the impact of project change requests.
Resolve Outstanding Issues To identify, track and resolve outstanding issues.
Verify Deliverable Compliance To verify the quality and process approvals of deliverables.
Maintain Project Configuration Item List To maintain the project's deliverables status list.
Report to Client Authority and to BRILLIO Management To provide the project status information to the client authority and BRILLIO Management.
Perform Milestone Reviews and Focus Synergy Sessions To conduct milestone reviews and synergy sessions according to the Project Plan.
Manage Human Resources To ensure effective integration and support of team members.
Perform Team Members Performance Appraisals To perform each team member's performance appraisal.
